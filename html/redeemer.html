CHRIST THE REDEEMER

INFORMATION

A statue of Jesus Christ called The Christ Redeemer may be found in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. It is also referred to as Cristo Redentor in Portuguese. One of the most famous sites in Brazil, the statue is 98 feet tall and perches atop Corcovado Mountain. With assistance from French engineer Albert Caquot, Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa erected the statue, which was created by French sculptor Paul Landowski. Since its dedication on October 12, 1931, the statue has come to represent Christianity and Brazilian patriotism.

The Christ Redeemer statue, which depicts Jesus with extended arms and represents the acceptance and love of God for all people, is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone. The statue is a popular destination for tourists from throughout the world and serves as a symbol of peace. The statue is lit up at night and is visible from numerous locations in Rio de Janeiro. One of the New Seven Wonders of the World, The Christ Redeemer is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
