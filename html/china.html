THE GREAT WALL OF CHINA

INFORMATION

The Great Wall of China is a network of defences made of brick, stone, tamped earth, and other materials that were typically constructed along an east-to-west line across China's northern borders to defend the Chinese states and empires from raids and incursions by various nomadic tribes of the Eurasian Steppe. With a length of more than 13,000 miles, the Great Wall is the world's longest wall. The Great Wall was built starting in the 7th century BCE, and over the centuries it saw extensions and renovations from different dynasties.

One of the Seven Wonders of the Medieval World, the Great Wall was named a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987. Millions of tourists visit it every year, making it one of the most well-known tourist destinations in China. The Great Wall is a monument to the creativity and tenacity of the Chinese people and provides a window into China's rich historical and cultural legacy. Despite its historical importance and iconic reputation, much of the Great Wall has deteriorated over time, and efforts are now being made to preserve and repair it.

LOCATION

The Great Wall of China is a series of walls that stretch from Liaoning through Hebei, Tianjin, Beijing, Inner Mongolia, Shanxi, Shaanxi, and Ningxia into Gansu within the country of China.
